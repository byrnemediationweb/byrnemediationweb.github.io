Why choose mediation?

Mediation is an alternative dispute resolution process. The mediator (who is a neutral third party) will attempt to assist the parties in exploring matters related to their dispute and further attempt to arrive at a mutually acceptable resolution. The mediator has no authority to impose a decision on the disputing parties. The mediator will assist the parties in exploring matters related to the dispute and exploring settlement possibilities in attempting to arrive at a mutually acceptable resolution to the dispute.

Mediation vs Litigation

Mediation Litigation
Who decides? You! A Judge.
Is the process confidential? Yes, it is private. No, it is public.
Result Both parties walk away with a share of the solution. One party may recieve nothing.
Who is involved? Two parties, their legal teams, and a mediator. Two parties, their legal teams, and a judge.
How long to resolve the dispute? The dispute can be resolved very quickly if both parties act in good faith to resolve the issue. The agreement could be signed at the end of one full day session. Very lengthy! It can take months or even years to resolve.
